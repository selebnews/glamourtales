1. The History of Page Six

Page Six has its roots in the New York Post, one of the oldest newspapers in the United States. Established in 1801, the New York Post introduced Page Six in 1977 as a gossip column to cater to readers' growing fascination with celebrity culture. The column quickly gained popularity and became a staple of the newspaper, known for its bold and often sensational headlines.

Over the years, Page Six has evolved into more than just a gossip column. It has become a brand synonymous with entertainment news, expanding its reach through online platforms and social media. As the landscape of celebrity reporting changed, Page Six adapted and grew, attracting a diverse audience that craved insider information about the lives of their favorite stars.
